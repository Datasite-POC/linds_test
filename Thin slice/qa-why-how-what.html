01 / Why
The Problem
  • Setup is too complex for medium and smaller deals. Admins must configure teams, categories, workflows, limits, and routing before a single question can be asked. Many skip it and default to email or Excel.
  • Document requests get lost in the question queue. There's no way to distinguish a question that needs an uploaded file from one that needs a text reply.
  • Upload isn't a natural answer path. Answer teams often respond with text or upload outside Q&A — missing the chance to create a tracked, Q&A-driven upload.
  • Bidders can't always access documents shared in answers. The resulting support escalation erodes trust in the workflow.

Phase 1 goal: Increase Active Q&A adoption (projects that reach 10 or more questions asked) by reducing the setup, classification, answer, and permissions barriers that prevent projects from reaching that threshold.

Each initiative is scoped to one moment in the Q&A lifecycle and designed to ship independently. They reinforce each other: categorisation makes the upload action more targeted, and the upload action is safer because permissions are confirmed.

03 / What
The Four Initiatives
01
Q&A Quick Launch
A simplified setup path using safe defaults so admins can launch Q&A without configuring custom workflows. Advanced setup remains available for complex deals.
02
Question Categorisation
A lightweight question type field that lets teams flag Document Requests. Answer teams can filter, prioritise, and reclassify.
03
Upload Document Answer Action
Upload becomes a first-class action in the Q&A answer flow, especially for Document Request questions. Answer teams no longer need to leave Q&A to attach a file.
04
Q&A Document Permissions
Before submitting an answer, users can confirm or set visibility for the intended bidder. A warning appears when access isn't yet granted.
